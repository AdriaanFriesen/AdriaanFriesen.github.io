Les Catacombs are a set of underground rooms and tunnels in Paris to store the contents of its overflowing cemetaries.

L'histoire

At the end of the 11th century, Paris's central cemetary was overflowing with bodies. To solve this, bones from the long dead were packed into walls of cemetary galleries. By the late 1800's however, Paris decided to finally deal with its overflowing dead once and (hopefully) for all. Paris had lots of empty mines from when it was using the limestone underground for buildings. They cleaned out the mineshafts and started putting bones underground. Another of Paris's motives to put bones underground was reducing contamination in the water supply caused by water flowing through the cemetaries and into Parisian water.

Aujourd'hui

Today, Les Catacombs are open to tourism. Tickets cost €27 (around $31 USD), and they are open to visits year-round. Once you have arrived at the entrance, you are greeted by a sign that says "Arrête! C'est ici l'empire de la Mort" ("Stop! This is the empire of Death") as you descend down the staircase.

In Les Catacombs, there are rooms and hallways with walls made entirely of bones. This was done so that the space could utilised as efficient as possible to prevent Paris running out of space for bodies again. Les Catacombs spans a massive 11 square kilometers of total area, and to walk the whole thing is a 1.5 kilometer hike.
